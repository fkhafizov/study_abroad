# RESULTS:
  
I checked current **official 2026 pages**. I did **not** find a Spain summer program explicitly labeled **accounting**; the strongest matches are **business, economics, finance, international business, marketing, and Spanish-language study**. For a U.S. junior in accounting who speaks English and wants to improve Spanish, these are the best fits I found. ([CIEE][1])

**1. CIEE Summer Business + Culture — Barcelona**
This is the strongest direct match for an accounting/business student. She takes **one business course per session** in **English** and can add **Communicative Spanish**. Summer 2026 options are **4 weeks** each (**May 18–June 15**, **June 15–July 13**, **July 13–August 8**), or **8 / 12 weeks** by combining sessions. Eligibility is **2.5 GPA**, plus prior coursework in areas such as **economics, accounting, finance, management, marketing, or statistics**. Credit is **3–4 U.S. semester credits per session**. Cost is **$4,950** for 4 weeks, **$8,910** for 8 weeks, or **$12,870** for 12 weeks, and that fee includes **housing**. The current application deadline listed is **April 1, 2026**. ([CIEE][1])

**2. UAM Summer School of Economics and Business — Madrid**
This is the best **university-run** business/econ option I found. It runs **June 29–July 24, 2026** for **4 weeks**, in **English**, with courses including **International Finance**, **Global Marketing**, **Doing Business in Spain**, and **Environmental Economics**. It is aimed at **Economics or Business** undergraduates or graduates, with **B2 English recommended**. She can earn **6 or 12 ECTS**. Tuition is **€1,599** for **1 course / 6 ECTS** or **€2,199** for **2 courses / 12 ECTS** for non-partners; **room and board are not included**. The **regular deadline is June 5, 2026** and the **extraordinary deadline is June 26, 2026**. UAM also notes that **International Finance** and **Global Marketing** were already **almost full** when that application page was captured. ([Summer School of Economics and Business][2])

**3. ESCI-UPF Barcelona Summer School — Barcelona**
This is a good fit if she wants **international business** in a shorter, flexible format. The program is modular: **1 to 4 weeks**, with the full run from **June 29–July 24, 2026**. It is taught **entirely in English** and covers topics like **sustainable entrepreneurship**, **business opportunities in Europe**, and **cross-cultural negotiation**. It is aimed at undergraduates, ideally with **two years completed**, and each week gives **3 ECTS**, up to **12 ECTS** total. Fees are **€150 registration**, plus **€550 / €900 / €1,450 / €1,750** for **1 / 2 / 3 / 4 weeks**. **Accommodation is not included.** Students receive a **Certificate of Completion** and an **official Transcript of Records**. The page says the applications portal **opened on March 16, 2026**. ([ESCI-UPF][3])

**4. UPF Barcelona International Summer School (BISS) — Barcelona**
This is the best **low-cost university option** if she is fine with a maximum of **4 weeks**. BISS runs **June 29–July 24, 2026**, offers a **Business & Economy** track, and courses are available in **English or Spanish**. International students can apply in **March, April, May**, with **late admissions June 1–7, 2026**. Fees for international students are **€150 registration**, then **€566.50** for a **1-week** course, **€772.50** for a **2-week** course, and **€927** for a **4-week** course; there are also discounted bundles for multiple courses. BISS provides an **official academic transcript**, but **accommodation is not included**. ([Universitat Pompeu Fabra][4])

**5. IES Abroad Salamanca Summer Language & Culture (Session II) — Salamanca**
This is the best option if her priority is **Spanish immersion**. Session II runs **July 1–July 30, 2026**. It gives **5 credits**, has **no language prerequisite**, and includes **housing options** in a **homestay or residence hall**. The listed **estimated cost** is **$5,585**. Instruction is in **Spanish**. This one is less business-focused, but it is a strong choice for building Spanish fast in a famous university city. The listed application deadline is **April 9, 2026**. For reference, **Session I** (**June 1–July 1**) had the same cost and format, but its page shows **Application Closed** with a **March 26, 2026** deadline. ([IES Abroad][5])

My recommendation, in order:

**Best overall for her major:** **CIEE Barcelona Business + Culture**. ([CIEE][1])
**Best lower-cost business option:** **UAM Summer School of Economics and Business**. 
**Best pure Spanish-immersion option:** **IES Salamanca Session II**. ([IES Abroad][5])
**Best budget university option for exactly 4 weeks:** **UPF BISS**. ([Universitat Pompeu Fabra][6])

On the **visa** question: Spain’s consular pages say that **study stays of 90 days or less do not require a study visa**, though a short-stay Schengen visa may be required for some nationalities; for a **U.S. citizen**, Spain’s travel page says **no visa is required for stays under 90 days**.
